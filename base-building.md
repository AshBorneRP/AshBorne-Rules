# 🏰 7. Compounds & Raiding

On AshBorne RolePlay, constructing a base is about establishing your footprint in the world, not hoarding weapons. A fortress must generate roleplay and serve a narrative purpose. 

Because of this, raiding is treated as a major server event. It is not a casual loot-run. Breaking into someone's home should shift the balance of power, spark long-term vendettas, and create incredible stories. Your goal as a raider is to leave an impact, not just to empty their crates.

### ⛺ 7.1 Compound Classifications & Limits
A base is defined as any structure that has been fortified (walls, gates, locks) to claim territory and keep the public out. 
* **One Base Limit:** Solos and groups are permitted exactly one primary fortified base. 
* **Factions:** Factions operate as a single unit and share a collective lock limit. Faction members must rely on each other. However, each member is allowed **one** personal codelock for their own private stash. 
* Factions are allowed one small forward outpost. If the faction expands into a secondary settlement, the outpost must be dismantled.
* *Note: Using alternate accounts to bypass these limits and build secret stashes will result in a permanent ban.*

#### 🔒 7.1.1 Security Limits (Codelocks)
* **Main Bases & Faction Settlements:** Capped at a maximum of **10 codelocks** (this covers all gates, doors, and secured containers).
* **Settler Housing:** Individual civilian homes built within a larger faction settlement are granted **3 codelocks** per home.

#### 🏗️ 7.1.2 Construction Requirements
* **RaG Basebuilding** is the mandatory mod for all major compound construction.
* Vanilla DayZ fences are only permitted to be used as gates in very specific scenarios where the RaG gates physically cannot snap into place.

### 🛑 7.2 Roleplay Overrides Game Mechanics
The DayZ engine allows you to do many things that make zero sense in a realistic roleplay environment. "The game let me do it" is not a valid defense on this server.
* You cannot dismantle a wall from the inside just to escape a compound you are locked inside of. That is exploiting mechanics. 
* You can technically hacksaw a codelock in-game, but on AshBorne, this is classified as a **Hard Raid** and is illegal without staff approval.
* *Rule of thumb: If your action relies on a video game mechanic rather than realistic logic, don't do it.*

### 🔓 7.3 Infiltrations (Soft Raiding)
A Soft Raid is a non-destructive break-in. It means you bypassed a base's security without breaking locks, destroying walls, or exploiting bugs. **Staff approval is not required for a Soft Raid.**

**🟢 Legal Soft Raid Tactics:**
* Walking through an accidentally unlocked gate, an open door, or a missing wall panel.
* Looting from unsecured, easily accessible storage.
* Leaving calling cards, writing notes, or stealing flags to initiate a rivalry.

**🔴 Illegal Soft Raid Tactics:**
* Abusing physics by stacking items (campfires, barrels) to vault over high walls.
* Player-boosting (jumping on a friend's head) or parking a truck next to a wall to jump over it.
* Glitching your character through a visually solid wall or window.
* Slapping your own codelock on an enemy's base, or setting traps inside their walls.
* Dismantling *anything* with tools. 

### 🧨 7.4 Destructive Breaches (Hard Raiding)
A Hard Raid involves the physical destruction of property (cutting locks, destroying walls, dismantling gates). 
**All Hard Raids require explicit approval via a Staff Ticket.** You must submit your request at least 48 hours in advance.

**Your Hard Raid Ticket Must Include:**
1. A massive, lore-accurate reason for the attack (revenge, territory disputes, ideology). Loot is not a reason.
2. The exact coordinates and screenshots of the target compound.
3. The names of the attackers and the defenders.
4. Your intended goal once you breach the walls.

#### ⚔️ 7.4.1 Hard Raid Execution
* If approved, you may breach exactly **one** exterior entry point. 
* Once inside, destroy only what is absolutely necessary to navigate the base. You may not destroy decorative roleplay items or non-blocking storage.
* Leave a trace. The victims must have a narrative thread to follow (graffiti, a note, a specific calling card).
* **You must record the entire raid** in case staff needs to review the footage.

### 🚫 7.5 Zero Tolerance for Griefing
We do not tolerate players who try to ruin the game for others out of out-of-character spite. 
**The following actions will result in bans and forced victim compensation:**
* Raiding the exact same base multiple times in a single week.
* Purposefully throwing loot on the ground so it despawns.
* Sabotaging the victim's ability to rebuild.
* Taking over a victim's base by putting your own locks on their doors.

### 🔫 7.6 Combat Rules During a Raid
* **Defenders:** If you catch someone actively destroying your base (cutting locks, breaking walls), they have initiated a hostile act. You may open fire without speaking. 
* **Defenders (Soft Raids):** If you catch someone sneaking in through an open door, you **must** verbally initiate before shooting them, unless they aim at you first.
* **Attackers:** If you are raiding the base, you may **never** shoot first without proper verbal initiation. 
* Standard No Value of Life (NVL) and fear RP rules apply the moment the raid begins.
